Millets

Millets are a group of small-seeded grains that belong to the Poaceae family, commonly grown in arid and semi-arid regions. Varieties include pearl millet, finger millet, foxtail millet, and barnyard millet. They are known for their high nutritional value, being rich in fiber, protein, vitamins, and minerals. Millets are gluten-free and have a low glycemic index, making them a healthy choice for those with dietary restrictions or diabetes. They can be cooked as a grain, used in porridge, or ground into flour for various recipes. Millets are also environmentally sustainable, requiring less water and resources compared to other staple crops.

Nuts

Nuts are hard-shelled fruits or seeds that are high in healthy fats, protein, vitamins, and minerals. Common types include almonds, walnuts, cashews, and hazelnuts. They are often consumed raw, roasted, or as nut butter, and are popular for their crunchy texture and rich, savory flavor. Nuts are also associated with various health benefits, such as heart health and weight management, due to their nutrient density..

Food

Food refers to any substance consumed to provide nutritional support for the body. It is typically composed of carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, and minerals. Food serves various purposes, including sustaining energy, promoting growth, and maintaining overall health.
